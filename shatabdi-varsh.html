Shatabdi Varsh

Celebrating 100 years of organised Khadayata community life — 1912 to 2012 and beyond

100

Shatabdi Varsh — 1912 to 2012

A century of unity, education, culture, and community service. The Khadayata Samaj marked its centenary with year-long celebrations, commemorations, and a renewed commitment to the values that have guided us for a hundred years.

The Centenary

What is Shatabdi Varsh?

The landmark 100th anniversary of the Khadayata Samaj organisation

A Century of Service to the Community

In 1912, a small group of visionary Khadayata community members in Bombay came together to establish the first formal Khadayata Samaj organisation. A century later, in 2012, the community celebrated this extraordinary milestone — the Shatabdi Varsh.

The Shatabdi Varsh was not merely a celebration of the past; it was a reaffirmation of the community's commitment to education, cultural preservation, welfare, and the spiritual values that have defined the Khadayata identity for over 700 years.

Events were held across Gujarat and beyond, bringing together thousands of Khadayata families to commemorate a century of organised community life, honour community leaders, and chart the vision for the next hundred years.

Shatabdi 2012

First Khadayata Parishad — Nadiad, 1914

The roots of the Shatabdi Varsh lie in the very first Khadayata Parishad, convened in Nadiad in 1914. Delegates from across Gujarat gathered to shape the future of the community — a tradition that continues to this day.

The Shatabdi Varsh Parishad of 2012 echoed that founding spirit, drawing delegates and community members from India and across the world.

🏛 1912 → 2012 → 2112 onward
100 Years of Progress

Centenary Achievements

What the Khadayata Samaj accomplished in its first century

🎓

Kelavani Mandal

The Khadayata Education Society (est. 1916) has disbursed crores of rupees in scholarships and interest-free educational loans over 100 years, enabling thousands of students to achieve higher education.

🏠

Khadayata Bhavans

Community centres established in Bombay, Ahmedabad, Baroda, and other major cities provided housing and support for Khadayata members migrating to urban areas for education and work.

🛕

Religious Facilities

The Samaj supported the establishment and maintenance of religious facilities and temples at Mahudi, Gokul, and Nathdwara — important pilgrimage sites for the Khadayata community.

👩

Women's Empowerment

The Mahila Mandal has run programmes for women's advancement, skill training, and social participation — ensuring women are integral to the community's progress.

📰

Khadayata Jyoti Magazine

The magazine has served as the community's voice for decades — publishing news, articles, member directories, and keeping the Khadayata family connected across India and the world.

🤝

Charitable Trust

The Janata Charitable Trust has provided welfare support to community members in need — financial assistance, medical support, and disaster relief over the decades.
