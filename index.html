About Us

About Khadayata Jyoti

A beacon of knowledge, culture, and community for the Khadayata Samaj for over a century.

The Voice of the Khadayata Community

Khadayata Jyoti is the official magazine and community portal serving the Khadayata Samaj — a proud Vania community with roots in North Gujarat, India. Our publication brings together news, articles, member directories, cultural insights, and community announcements for families across India and the globe.

Founded alongside the first formal Khadayata Samaj organization in 1912, our magazine has been an unbroken thread connecting generations of Khadayata families — whether in their ancestral villages or living far across the world.

We publish community news, religious guidance, scholarship information, articles on culture and heritage, and maintain a comprehensive directory of Khadayata members.

Khadayata Samaj

A 700-Year-Old Legacy

The Khadayata are a distinguished Vania caste within the Vaishnav Hindu tradition, originating from the village of Khadat in North Gujarat approximately 700 years ago.

Known globally for their business acumen, deep family values, and devotion to Shree Nathji, the Khadayata community has flourished across India and all continents.

🏛 Khadayata Parishad — Nadiad, 1914
Our History

Journey Through Time

Key milestones in the history of the Khadayata Samaj

Origins in Khadat Village

The Khadayata community traces its origins to the village of Khadat in North Gujarat. Early families were commodity traders, eventually invited by King Mulraj Solanki to settle across Gujarat.

~1300
AD
1912

First Khadayata Samaj Organisation

Community members in Bombay established the first formal "Khadayata Samaj" organization, marking the beginning of organised community life.

First Khadayata Parishad

The inaugural Khadayata Parishad (grand convention) was held in Nadiad, Gujarat, attended by delegates from across the state. This became the cornerstone of community governance.

1914
1916

Khadayata Kelavani Mandal Founded

The Khadayata Education Society (Kelavani Mandal) was established to provide scholarships and interest-free loans to students, significantly advancing education within the community.

Khadayata Jyoti Magazine Launched

The Khadayata Jyoti magazine was established as the community's official publication — connecting members across India with news, culture, and community updates.

Est.
2012

Shatabdi Varsh — Centenary Celebrations

The Khadayata Samaj celebrated 100 years of organised community life with the Shatabdi Varsh — a landmark year of events, commemorations, and renewed dedication to community values.

Global Digital Presence

Khadayatajyoti.org launched to extend the reach of the magazine online, connecting Khadayata families worldwide through a digital platform for news, directories, and forms.

2013

Pushti Marg

Ashta Darshan of Shree Nathji

The eight daily Darshans (holy viewings) of Lord Shree Nathji — the central devotional practice of the Khadayata community's Pushti Marg tradition

1
Mangla
Early Morning
The morning awakening of the Lord — the first and most auspicious darshan of the day
2
Shringar
Morning
The Lord is adorned with beautiful garments and ornaments for the day's worship
3
Gwal
Mid Morning
The pastoral darshan — the Lord prepares to tend to the divine cows of Vrindavan
4
Rajbhog
Midday
The grand midday meal offering — the most elaborate of all the daily darshans
5
Utthanpan
Afternoon
The Lord awakens from His midday rest, refreshed and ready for afternoon devotions
6
Bhog
Evening
The evening meal offering with specially prepared dishes and sweets for the Lord
7
Sandhya
Dusk
The evening darshan — the Lord returns with the divine cows at the close of day
8
Shayan
Night
The bedtime darshan — the Lord is prepared for rest, concluding the day's worship

🛕 Primary temples: Mahudi · Gokul · Nathdwara
